George Carlin

The man who changed stand-up comedy forever
“That's why they call it the American Dream, because you have to be asleep to believe it.”

    A little bit of George Carlin's life:

  • 1937 - George Denis Patrick Carlin was born in Manhattan, New York.
  • 1959 - He met Jack Burns and formed a very succesful comedy team.
  • 1967 - Releases his first live debut album 'Take-Offs and Put-Ons'.
  • 1972 - Releases AM & FM, album that marked Carlin's change from mainstream to counterculture comedy.
  • 1975 - He hosted the premiere broadcast of NBC's Saturday Night Live.
  • 1976 - Carlin unexpectedly stopped performing regularly, when his career appeared to be at its height.
  • 1981 - He returned to the stage, releasing 'A Place for My Stuff' and returning to HBO and New York City with the Carlin at Carnegie TV special, videotaped at Carnegie Hall and airing during the 1982–83 season.
  • 1991 - Carlin began to achieve prominence as a film actor with a major supporting role in the comedy hit Outrageous Fortune, starring Bette Midler and Shelley Long.
  • 1993 - He began a weekly Fox sitcom, The George Carlin Show, playing New York City taxicab driver George O'Grady.
  • 1997 - Carlin was honored at the Aspen Comedy Festival with a retrospective, George Carlin: 40 Years of Comedy, hosted by Jon Stewart.
  • 2001 - Carlin was given a Lifetime Achievement Award at the 15th Annual American Comedy Awards.
  • 2008 - Carlin died on June 22, at Saint John's Health Center in Santa Monica, California, of cardiac arrest at age 71.
